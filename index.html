Now for a question. What is interactivity?

If you want a really technical answer, the definition of "interactive" (according to the Oxford Dictionary) means:
1. Reciprocally active; acting upon or influencing each other.
  or
2. Pertaining to or being a computer or other electronic device that llows a two-way flow of information between and a user, responding immediately to the latter input.

If I wanted to make this button interactive, does it mean that it needs to give you a response once you press it? If the button didn't give you an immediate feedback response, would it just be static and "non-interactive"? This button could just exist and give you nothing in return (except for that sweet, sweet effect when you hover in and out of its border).


But this Oxford definition can be very easily re-interpreted and applied into different circumstances.

"The screen forms a bridge between our bodies and the realm of circuits and electricity inside computers." (source, please read the introduction - it's actually quite interesting). This screen is displaying this website which is displaying text that I've written.

It is a bridge between my thoughts and your eyes and thus is exchanging information, an interaction, between the two of us.
